# Philosophy

## Why We Are Doing This

Voynich Lab exists to make research work reviewable, reproducible, and resistant to self-deception. The purpose is not to announce that the Voynich Manuscript has been solved, but to test hypotheses in a disciplined way.

## What Counts as Progress

Progress includes:

- a reproducible experiment;
- a better documented dataset;
- a clarified hypothesis;
- a negative result that rules out a weak idea;
- a recorded contradiction between experiments;
- a source-backed correction.

## What Does Not Count as Proof

A visual similarity, isolated example, or plausible story is not proof. A result should not be treated as strong unless it survives corpus-level checks, section-level checks, transcription choices, and simple baselines.

## Main Risks

- cherry-picking;
- pattern-seeking after seeing results;
- changing protocols after results are known;
- confusing observations with interpretation;
- relying on unsourced historical or bibliographic claims.

## Protection Against Self-Deception

Use explicit labels:

- `OBS` for observations;
- `INF` for interpretations;
- `HYP` for hypotheses;
- `DEC` for decisions;
- `TODO` for next actions;
- `ERR` for mistakes or rejected ideas.

## Role of AI

AI can help organize documentation, write reproducible code, propose checks, and summarize sessions. AI must not independently finalize major interpretation or claim a decipherment.

## Role of Humans

Humans approve major interpretive changes, choose research priorities, evaluate sources, and decide whether a hypothesis should be marked as rejected, supported, or superseded.
