# Glossary

## Voynich Manuscript

A medieval manuscript commonly called the Voynich Manuscript. Stable factual details must be source-backed before use in analysis. Status: `needs-source`.

## Voynichese

The conventional name for the manuscript's undeciphered text or writing system. Status: `needs-source`.

## EVA

European Voynich Alphabet, a transcription system used by researchers to represent Voynich glyphs in Latin characters. Status: `needs-source`.

## Glyph

A written sign or character-like unit.

## Token

A whitespace- or rule-delimited unit in a transcription.

## Type

A unique token form.

## Vord

A common term for a Voynichese word-like token. Status: `needs-source`.

## Folio

A manuscript leaf, usually referenced by recto and verso sides.

## Quire

A group of manuscript leaves gathered together. Status: `needs-source`.

## Currier A/B

Labels associated with textual varieties described in Voynich research. Status: `needs-source`.

## Gallows Character

A term often used for several tall Voynich glyphs. Status: `needs-source`.

## Entropy

A statistical measure of uncertainty or information content.

## Positional Constraint

A pattern where a glyph, token, or feature appears preferentially in certain positions.

## Section

A content grouping such as botanical, astronomical/astrological, balneological/biological, pharmaceutical, or recipes/text-only. Status: `needs-source`.

## Scribe / Hand

A proposed distinction between writing hands in the manuscript. Status: `needs-source`.

## Transcription

A machine-readable representation of manuscript text.
